Key Assumptions & Disclosures

  • Annual annuity payments are made at the end of each year
  • Investment growth is applied to the opening balance for the year
  • The grantor pays income taxes outside the trust — trust balance is unaffected (key benefit of grantor CLAT structure)
  • The §7520 rate is the rate in effect at trust creation; grantor may elect either of the two preceding months' rates
  • The model assumes a fixed investment return each year with no market volatility
  • The upfront income tax deduction equals the PV of the charitable annuity stream, subject to 30%/60% of AGI limits with a 5-year carryforward (IRC §170)
  • Governed by IRC §2055(e)(2)(B) for gift/estate tax treatment and IRC §170 for grantor income tax deduction
  • This tool is for educational and planning purposes only — not legal or tax advice. Consult a qualified estate planning attorney and CPA.
